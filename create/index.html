Step 5

Domain validation (TXT)

Since we may be hosing many domains, we have to validate that you own the domain. To do so, we ask you to add a simple TXT record for the root domain. This allow us to validate your domain and to link it with your account.

Note: replace acentera.com with your own domain name.
Let say the website you want to build is : wwww.acentera.com, you will have to create a TXT Record for acentera-websites.acentera.com. You will have to log into your DNS Hosting provider and add the acentera-websites.acentera.com TXT "v=xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx" as seen in the picture below. The double quotes are importants.


Prior clicking the "Validate TXT", you might want to validate that your TXT Record was properly updated. Here are two command line examples you can use.

Example using the linux Dig Command:
dig -t txt +short acentera-websites.acentera.com
"v=c4ca4238a0b923820dcc509a6fXXXXXX"

Example using the nslookup:
nslookup -type=TXT acentera-websites.acentera.com
Somewhere in the answer you should see "v=c4ca4238a0b923820dcc509a6fXXXXXX"

Note: In your case you will have a different hash for your own websites. Every hash are unique per accounts.

After clicking on the Validate TXT button, If we were able to successfully validate that you are the owner of that domain, the following button will appear. Simply click on it.



Step 6

Domaine Change (CNAME)

Once the TXT Record has successfully been validated, we have successfully configured and are ready to be your next hosting solution.
In short, if you have an active HTTP domain end-user will not notice downtime if this step is properly executed.

You will have to log into your DNS Hosting provider, and this time add the CNAME entry as seen in the picture below.



Depending on your provider configuration, this step may take few hours.
Prior clicking the "Validate DNS Record", you may want to validate the DNS has properly updated with your changes.

Once ready, you simply have to click on the "Validate DNS Record" and we will automatically create the SSL Certificates and assign it to your website. This step may take up to 30 seconds.
